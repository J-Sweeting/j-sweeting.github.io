James Sweeting

Researcher and Lecturer in Game Studies

About Me

James Sweeting is a Programme Leader and a Lecturer in Game Studies at the University of Plymouth. Also teaches on MA Game Design, BA Game Arts and Design, and BA Digital Media Design (Games Design) [in partnership with Nanjing University of the Arts].

He has completed a PhD with Transtechnology Research (University of Plymouth) investigating the hauntological form of videogames. This is supported via an understanding of nostalgia present across the medium itself and the relationship it has with the past.

In addition, he is an Associate Fellow of The Higher Education Academy, having attained recognition for meeting the UK Professional Standards Framework for teaching and learning support in higher education.

You can read his thoughts around his research topics and videogames by reading his blog titled Relative Nostlgia and his Medium publication also titled Relative Nostalgia.

Also provides book reviews for Leonardo. With work having appeared in the main academic journal. As well as contributing to and editing the Transtechnology Research Reader publication.

Previously graduated with an MA in War Studies from King’s College London and a BSc (Hons) in International Politics from City, University of London.

In his spare time as a street photographer, he captures the life of the residents of Plymouth.
